### West Wales Catchment Partnerships –<br>Teifi (Ceredigion)

- *Hybrid. Join online: [Meeting Link](https://teams.microsoft.com/meet/312077779953060?p=lctcsLD1VlF6d2vRAQ)*

A stakeholder meeting to discuss the **possibilities and opportunities for developing a Catchment Partnership for the Teifi (Ceredigion)**.

The **West Wales Nutrient Management Board (WWNMB)**, working in partnership with the **Ceredigion Local Nature Partnership (CLNP)**, would like to invite the **current Teifi Nutrient Management Board stakeholders**, along with other interested individuals, groups, organisations and businesses, to come together and discuss the next steps towards establishing a catchment partnership.

The aim is to build on the existing **groups, projects and activity across the catchment**, bringing together those with an interest in the riverine environment and those living and working within it.
### Purpose of the Catchment Partnership
Catchment partnerships form part of a wider move towards **locally led, collaborative delivery**, linking environmental priorities across sectors and supporting coordinated action at catchment scale.

The aim of the catchment partnership is to bring together the many people and projects currently working for the benefit of the **River Teifi and its tributaries** and to:
- Facilitate better networking and collaboration
- Support a more **holistic ‘source to sea’ approach**
- Improve delivery of conservation and nutrient mitigation actions
- Strengthen links between local delivery and strategic decision-making
- Identify shared learning and future funding opportunities
### Aims of this meeting
The main aims of the meeting are to:
- Identify the **key stakeholders** that should be involved going forward
- Discuss the **potential scope and focus** of the partnership
- Begin to shape a **roadmap for future development**
- Consider an **interim approach to representation**
- Nominate **two representatives to attend WWNMB Board meetings** during the initial development phase

### 👥 Who should attend?
This session is open to stakeholders with an interest in:
- Water quality and nutrient management
- Rivers, habitats and biodiversity
- Land management and agriculture
- Community and citizen science
- Environmental delivery and policy

We are particularly keen to hear from individuals and organisations working across the **Teifi catchment**.
### 🌊 Why attend?
This is a key opportunity to:
- Help shape how the **Teifi Catchment Partnership develops**
- Ensure local knowledge and priorities are reflected

- Contribute to a more joined-up, **“source to sea” approach**

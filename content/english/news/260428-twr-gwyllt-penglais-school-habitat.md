We began by slowing things down and asking some simple but important questions: what is a Tŵr Gwyllt, who is it for, and what could this place become. The group spent time observing what is already here, thinking about what we can see and what we can’t, and noticing the different forms of life that might call this space home.

From there, ideas started to open out. There was a strong feeling that this shouldn’t just be a structure for wildlife, but also a calm place where people can connect, sit, notice, and simply be. A space for contemplation alongside habitat.

We moved into making, using simple materials like wooden blocks and the construction kits to test out forms and structures. This gave everyone a way to think through scale, shape, and how the tower might hold different species from insects and birds to small mammals while also holding people in a quieter, more reflective way.

There’s already been a real sense of curiosity and ownership building. The group are beginning to see this not just as a project, but as something they are shaping together, something that will live here and continue beyond the sessions.

Next, we’ll start refining the design and thinking more practically about materials, structure, and how it comes together on site.

Coed Y Bont Community Woodland, 24.2 hectares, near Pontrhydfendigaid, is owned by the Welsh Government, managed by Natural Resources Wales (NRW), of which we have a management agreement.

In 2008 a group of people in Pontrhydfendigaid (known as 'Bont') were inspired by Al Gore’s film 'The Inconvenient Truth' which encouraged people to plant trees and in a very small way it was felt that Bont could make a contribution in its own corner of the planet.

Two woodlands known as Coed Dolgoed and Coed Cnwch, situated on the edge of Pontrhydfendigaid and the historic site of Strata Florida Abbey, was identified as a great opportunity to develop and create a community woodland. The conifers, planted in Coed Dolgoed (1950s) were felled in 2005 and the ground started to recolonise, but it was felt that there could be some replanting with native broadleaves.Further conifers were felled in 2021 and 10,000 of mainly broadleaved trees planted in 2022 by NRW. We were involved in organising a tree health day, advising on repairing and development of paths and selecting planted species.

Coed Y Bont’s management of the woodland is based on access, education, biodiversity and engagement of the community. We have created a great place to connect with nature and enhance health and wellbeing by a combination of being awarded various sized grants for different projects, forming relationships with various organisations, organising open days and events based on biodiversity and nature, encouraging outdoor education of children and and carrying out monthly volunteer sessions.We have won 4 awards, including the Community Green Flag Award since 2016, built up various patnerships with wildlife organisations and became one of the first 14 National Forests in 2020.

Wildlife cameras were deployed in the Clywedog forest by Roger and Carol Daniel. The cameras were successful in recording red squirrels and other wildlife. Recordings will be posted on the Community Council website, and shared with the Ceredigion Council Biodiversity Team and the Wildlife trust of South and West Wales.

Recordings of wildlife on fixed cameras distributed in parts of the forest. The project is ongoing while the cameras are functioning and all wildlife will be logged.

Roger Daniel is a veterinary surgeon and worked in a veterinary diagnostic  laboratory in Aberystwyth. Carol Daniel was laboratory manager at the same laboratory. Both are now retired and devoted to this project. 

Roger is a member of the Llanfair Clydogau and Cellan Community Council and was successful in obtaining a grant, for the Community Council, from Ceredigion Council Biodiversity Team. With the grant, six wildlife cameras were purchased for this project. 

Roger and Carol were trained in use of the wildlife cameras, and they’re deployment, by Ben Allen of the South and West Wales Wildlife Trust. Roger and Carol continue to liaise with Ben, and are passionate about saving the last remaining colony of red squirrels in mid Wales. This colony is under threat from wind turbines planned for construction in the Clywedog forest.
